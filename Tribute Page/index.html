Steve Jobs

In memory of Steve Jobs, a man who changed the world with his revolutionary ideas.

  • Birth and Adoption: Born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, Steve Jobs was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. His biological parents were Joanne Schieble and Abdulfattah Jandali.
  • Education and Dropout: Jobs attended Reed College in Portland, Oregon but dropped out after six months, later auditing classes like calligraphy that influenced Apple's design ethos.
  • Apple's Founding: In 1976, Jobs co-founded Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne in his garage. Their first product, Apple I, laid the foundation for the personal computer industry.
  • Apple's Success: The launch of the Apple II in 1977 made Apple a leader in the personal computer market. The Macintosh, introduced in 1984, revolutionized the industry with its graphical user interface.
  • Departure from Apple: In 1985, Jobs was ousted from Apple after internal conflicts. He then founded NeXT, a computer platform development company.
  • Pixar Acquisition: Jobs bought the Graphics Group, later renamed Pixar, from Lucasfilm in 1986. Pixar produced hit movies like "Toy Story" and became a leading animation studio.
  • Return to Apple: Apple acquired NeXT in 1997, bringing Jobs back as CEO. He revitalized the company with products like the iMac, iPod, and the introduction of the Apple Store.
  • iPhone Revolution: The iPhone, launched in 2007, transformed the mobile phone industry and positioned Apple as a leader in consumer electronics.
  • Health Struggles: Jobs battled pancreatic cancer starting in 2004. Despite multiple medical leaves, he continued to lead Apple until his resignation in 2011.
  • Legacy and Death: Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011. His visionary leadership and innovative products have left a lasting impact on technology, design, and popular culture.
